# Basalt Chapel Below the Mire

The dungeon is a drowned chapel carved into black volcanic stone beneath a cold swamp. Narrow halls connect prayer cells, a flooded crypt, and a bell tower that leans sideways underground because the earth shifted centuries ago. Pale fungus grows along the walls, making the carved saints look as if they are weeping light. The central threat is a grave-bound knight who patrols by the sound of iron dragging across stone, while smaller dangers include leech pools, trapped reliquaries, and a hidden archive sealed behind a broken altar.
